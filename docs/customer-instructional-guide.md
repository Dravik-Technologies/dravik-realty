# Dravik Realty Command Center

Customer Instructional Guide

Version: Beta staging guide  
Updated: July 11, 2026

## Chapter 1: Welcome

Dravik Realty Command Center is a workspace for real estate teams that need one place to manage leads, clients, transactions, referrals, marketing, mortgage activity, reporting, team operations, and client portal access.

The platform has two separate experiences:

- **Command Center**: the internal workspace for brokers, realtors, lenders, admins, and team members.
- **Client Portal**: the client-facing workspace for buyers, sellers, and transaction clients.

Your brokerage may not use every module. Available modules depend on your subscription, role, and team setup.

## Chapter 2: Signing In

### Internal Users

Internal users sign in to the Command Center to manage business operations.

Typical internal users include:

- Broker owners
- Realtors
- Team leads
- Transaction coordinators
- Lending partners
- Admin staff

After signing in, you land on the Dashboard.

### Client Users

Clients do not use the internal Command Center. They use the separate Client Portal.

Client portal access is intended for:

- Transaction updates
- Documents
- Messages
- Saved properties
- Mortgage progress

Production client access is designed to use invitation-based access so clients only see their own portal.

## Chapter 11: Transactions

Transactions track deals from contract through closing.

Use Transactions to:

## Chapter 12: Client Portal Admin

Client Portal Admin is the internal realtor-facing management surface.

This area is not the client portal itself. It is where internal users manage portal readiness and client access status.

Use it to:

- Review client portal readiness

- Preview the client portal experience

Common workflow:

1. Open **Client Portal Admin**.
2. Review client access status.
3. Check document and message readiness.
4. Use **Preview Client Portal** to view the client-facing experience.

## Chapter 13: Client Portal

The Client Portal is the separate client-facing area.

Clients should only see:

- Their transaction status
- Their documents
- Their messages
- Their saved properties
- Their mortgage progress, when applicable

Clients should not see:

- Internal CRM
- Team management
- Brokerage reports
- Other clients
- Internal billing or settings

Recommended client workflow:

1. Client signs in through the client portal link.
2. Client reviews transaction progress.
3. Client checks needed documents.
4. Client reads or sends messages.
5. Client returns to the portal for updates instead of searching email threads.

## Chapter 20: Recommended Daily Workflow

For agents:

1. Start on Dashboard.
2. Check Unified Inbox.
3. Review Lead Engine & Smart CRM.
4. Work Prospecting, Listings, or Mapping as needed.
5. Update Transactions.
6. Confirm client portal readiness for active clients.

For brokers:

1. Start on Dashboard.
2. Review Reports & Analytics.
3. Check Team Management.
4. Review high-priority client, transaction, listing, and referral activity.
5. Open Settings only when administrative changes are needed.

For lenders:

1. Start on Dashboard.
2. Open Mortgage Tools.
3. Review pipeline and calculator workflows.
4. Coordinate status updates with the transaction and client portal.

For clients:

1. Sign in to the Client Portal.
2. Review current transaction status.
3. Check messages.
4. Review needed documents.
5. Return to the portal for updates.

## Chapter 21: Beta/Staging Notes

Staging is used for review before production release.

In staging:

- Data may be empty so teams can test workflows cleanly.
- Some production services, such as final customer authentication and live integrations, may be connected in later release steps.
- The interface should still be tidy, navigable, and free of broken controls.
- If something appears clickable, it should perform an action or open the relevant workflow.

When testing staging:

1. Visit every module in the sidebar.
2. Try the visible tabs, filters, and view toggles.
3. Confirm the client portal is separate from the internal Command Center.
4. Confirm the branding is correct.
5. Report any confusing copy, broken layout, or button that appears usable but does nothing.

## Chapter 23: Glossary

**Command Center**  
The internal workspace for your brokerage team.

**Client Portal**  
The separate client-facing workspace for transaction clients.

**Module**  
A major product area such as CRM, Marketing, Referrals, Mortgage Tools, or Reports.

**Pipeline**  
A workflow board or status sequence used to track progress.

**Entitlement**  
A module or feature included in your subscription plan.

**Staging**  
A pre-production environment used to test the product before release.

**Production**  
The live environment used by real customers and real business data.
